Dealership Services

Vehicle Sales

New Vehicles: Dealerships sell brand-new cars from specific manufacturers, often including the latest models with customizable features.

Used Vehicles: Many dealerships also sell pre-owned cars, often certified for quality and reliability through inspections.

Trade-In Services

Customers can trade in their current vehicles to offset the cost of a new or used car. Dealerships appraise the value of the trade-in based on its condition, mileage, and market demand.

Financing and Leasing

Financing: Dealerships partner with banks or lending institutions to provide car loans, often offering competitive interest rates and payment plans.

Leasing: They also offer leasing options, allowing customers to use a vehicle for a set period and return it afterward.

Vehicle Servicing and Repairs

Most dealerships have service centers staffed by certified technicians who perform maintenance like oil changes, brake replacements, tire rotations, and more extensive repairs.

Parts and Accessories Sales

Dealerships sell genuine OEM (Original Equipment Manufacturer) parts and accessories, ensuring compatibility and quality for repairs and customizations.

Vehicle Customization

Some dealerships provide customization services, such as adding features like upgraded sound systems, special paint jobs, or aftermarket performance enhancements.

Car Detailing

Comprehensive cleaning services, including interior and exterior detailing, are offered to keep vehicles in pristine condition.

Insurance Services

Many dealerships partner with insurance companies to provide auto insurance options, streamlining the process for customers purchasing or financing vehicles.

Roadside Assistance Programs

Some dealerships offer membership or packages that include roadside assistance services, such as towing, jump-starts, tire changes, and lockout assistance.
